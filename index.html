The Big Assumption

So the entire system relies on one big, fat assumption - that we can truthfuly answer this simple question:

"Are they doing a good job?"

Turns out that everyone tries to convince us that they either are doing a great job, or a terrible one. Lot's of disinformation flies around. What if there was a way to objectively evaluate the gov's performance? Turns out there is such a way.

    The structure of the budget

    The budget's structure is the result of political decisions

    Form follows Function - and vice versa!

    examples: - represented activities vs. spread activities - depth and supervision - the deeper the item is in the budget, the least supervision it requires. - tagalong activities - some government departments are political prizes which are tossed around between ministers and ministries.
    Grouping different budget items into themed groups allow us to compare entire activities and not single activities.

    • Previous commitments
    • New laws and regulation
    • Depends on various multipliers
    • Assumptions and projections
    • Global cap
